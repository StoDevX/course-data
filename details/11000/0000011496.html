PHIL 252: Ethics and the Good Life (2005-2006, Interim)

Students examine main Western ethical perspectives and how they apply to selected contemporary moral concerns. Theoretical issues include rights, duties, virtue, hedonism, egoism, the good life, ethics and theology, the fact-value distinction, relativism and pluralism. Students discuss current topics such as economic justice, death, friendship, animal rights, censorship, racism, privacy, reproductive ethics and environmental ethics.
