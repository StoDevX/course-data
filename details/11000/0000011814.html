ECON 244: Business, Government and the Marketplace (2005-2006, Semester 2)

Students in this course gain a critical understanding of several forms of government regulation by studying antitrust, old-style regulation and issues in consumer and workplace safety. Students examine such intervention from both historical and economic perspectives, by participating in oral and written cases arguing opposing sides on historical and modern issues ranging from Standard Oil (1911) to the regulation of nuclear power. Prerequisite: one of Economics 110-122 or consent of the instructor. Offered annually.
