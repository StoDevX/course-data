ECON 113: Capitalism: The Struggle of Economic Ideas in History (2006-2007, Summer Session 2)

This course uses major school of economic thought and major economic writers from the past 600 years to introduce principles of economics. (See Economics 121.) Readings include the architects of capitalism (Adam Smith, David Ricardo, etc.), the critics (Karl Marx, Thorstein Veblem, etc.), and the reformers (J.M. Keynes, Joseph Schumpeter, J.K. Galbraith, etc.). Offered many Interims.
