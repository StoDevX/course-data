REL 292: (2007-2008, Interim)

This course offers a study of the connection between Christian aesthetics and ethics. Students explore aesthetic experience, relating aesthetics, which includes art, architecture and music, with a theological anthropology that correlates aesthetics and ethics. Special attention is paid to norms that guide a Christian understanding of aesthetics and human action. Students participate in a number of off-campus research trips. Prerequisite: completion of BTS-T.
