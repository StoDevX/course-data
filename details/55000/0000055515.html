ECON 114: Economics of Human Resources (2006-2007, Interim)

This course focuses on the economics of human resources in the modern corporation. Managers today face many questions about their employment practices. This course addresses such issues from the perspective of both manager and worker. Students are introduced to theories of human capital, labor supply, and the economics of education. Specific topics include theory of wage differentials, labor market discrimination, hiring, promotion, evaluation, raiding, job definition, pay schemes, benefits, and design of work.
