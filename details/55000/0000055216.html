PSYCH 229: Child Development: Influences of Technology and Media (2006-2007, Interim)

This course explores how today's media and technologies shape child development. The course focuses on the effects of TV, video games, computers, toys, music, and other technologies on children's cognitive and social development. It also critically examines how the science of developmental psychology is presented in the media. The class considers how the media and popular press affect child development by influencing parents. Prerequisite: Psychology 125, Psychology 241 (recommended), Media Studies 160.
