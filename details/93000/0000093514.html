ENGL 269: Art, Design, and Literature in Britain Since 1950 (2013-2014, Semester 2)

This cross-disciplinary course traces the flourishing of British creativity in art, design, and literature from the postwar era to the present--a period in which Britain emerged as an influential nation in a number of creative fields, from music to fashion, product design to architecture. Students examine works, movements, creative practices, and critical methodologies from art, design, and literature in order to develop a panoramic understanding of contemporary British creativity. Prerequisite: FYW. Offered alternate years.
