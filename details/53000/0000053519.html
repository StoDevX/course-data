PSYCH 338: (2006-2007, Semester 2)

With recent advances in neuroscience, we can now describe the biological correlates of many psychological disorders, including schizophrenia, depression and anxiety disorders. In this discussion-based course, students use published research findings to examine the connections between the symptoms of these pathologies and changes in brain neurobiology, biochemistry, and physiology. Prerequisites: Psychology 125, 231, and 238, or Neuroscience 234 and Psychology 246, or consent of instructor.
