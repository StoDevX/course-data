ENGL 220: Topics in Literary History (2016-2017, Semester 2)

This course examines the history of nineteenth-century American literature by focusing on the topic of freedom. Students read works by writers who embraced AmericaÂ’s literary independence from Britain, the liberation of individuals from traditional institutions, and the freedom of women and people of color from various forms of oppression. Developments in such genres as the novel, the short story, the autobiographical narrative, and poetry will be considered. Writers studied include Emerson, Thoreau, Hawthorne, Melville, Whitman, Fern, Fuller, Jacobs, Douglass, Dickinson, Twain, James, Chesnutt, Crane, and Chopin. Prerequsite: FYW.
