PSYCH 390A: Issues in Psychology (2016-2017, Semester 2)

All children, adults, and families experience stressful life events. This seminar examines how stressors shape behavioral and biological development. We discuss psychological and physiological aspects of being "stressed." Example topics include: early life stress and neurobehavioral development, adolescent stress and coping, and the impact of stress on cognition, well-being, and health throughout the lifespan. Students explore interventions that aim to reduce stress-related risks and foster healthy development. Prerequisites: PSYCH 230, plus PSYCH 238 or NEURO 239, PSYCH 241 recommended
