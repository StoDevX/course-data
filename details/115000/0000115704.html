RACE 251: Multiculturalism and Public Education (2017-2018, Semester 2)

This class explores the influential contemporary social movement Black Lives Matter by investigating its historical origins and current condition. Readings consider the social and political context of the movement's birth through its civil rights antecedents, the Great Migration of African Americans from the rural South, and the theories behind and application of policing strategies like "stop and frisk." Special attention will be paid to the movement in Minneapolis and St. Paul, including the Fourth District police precinct occupation, trials of police officers and others, and the legal response from the cities and the Minnesota legislature. Offered periodically, topics vary. Counts toward Race and Ethnic Studies major and concentration.
