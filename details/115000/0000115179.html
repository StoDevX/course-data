ENGL 220: Topics in Literary History (2017-2018, Semester 1)

This course traces the development of American literature during one of the most eventful periods in American history. From the Civil War to the civil rights movement, from westward expansion to American imperialism, this course examines the poetry, fiction, and drama produced as America was rising to cultural and global dominance, while dealing with inequality at home. Potential authors include Twain, Wharton, Gilman, Crane, Hughes, Frost, Hurston, Faulkner, Miller, Ellison and Rich. Prerequisite: FYW.
