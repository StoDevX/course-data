GERM 272: Turning Points in German History (2005-2006, Semester 1)

Examines a major period of German history and its impact on the institutional, intellectual and artistic heritage of Europe. Close reading and analysis of primary sources and critical evaluations of the period. Focus on history as an interpretive reconstruction of the past. Sample periods include: the Reformation, Weimar Classicism, the German revolution of 1848, German modernism and post-holocaust Germany. Taught in German. Prerequisite: one 250-level course (two recommended). May be repeated if topic is different. GE: FOL-G, HWC.
