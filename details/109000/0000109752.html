REL 249: Modern Jewish Thought (2016-2017, Semester 2)

This course examines Jewish religious and philosophical thought in Europe, the United States, and Israel. Social/cultural history and literature provide additional lenses on the modern Jewish experience. Important themes include emancipation, integration, and acculturation; the Enlightenment and its challenges to the authority of Jewish religious tradition; the changing role of religion in the emergent secular nation-state; and negotiations of minority/majority status in modern societies. Prerequisite: sophomore status or higher. Open to first-year students with permission of the instructor.
