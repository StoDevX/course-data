NURS 140: Health and Social Inequality: Rural and Urban Perspectives (off campus (2015-2016, Interim)

Health begins where we live, work and play. Students are immersed in public, private, and community-based organizations in Northfield (rural) and Minneapolis-St. Paul (urban) to gainanunderstanding of the social determinants of health (environmental conditions, resourcesandsupports) and the relationship to individual health outcomes through service learning.Aspecialemphasis is placed on the impact of socioeconomic status, geographic location,anddisability onhealth disparities. Prerequisites: one course in sociology/anthropology, women'sandgenderstudies, social work, family studies, economics, political science, environmentalstudies,ornursing. Offered during Interim
