ENGL 340: Advanced Studies in Literary Eras: American (2016-2017, Interim)

This course focuses on American fiction of the late 19th and early 20th centuries that reflects changing attitudes towards gender roles; sexual identity and behavior; and marriage, motherhood, and divorce. Students read works of fiction by Henry James, Kate Chopin, Charlotte Perkins Gilman, Edith Wharton, and others, and analyze these texts in the context of the cultural and literary controversies they generated.
