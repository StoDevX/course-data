GREEK 370: Topics in Greek Literature (2012-2013, Semester 1)

Students translate selections from one or more genres of ancient Greek literature while exploring a specific topic or theme chosen by the instructor. Close study of the text is combined with discussion of broader literary, historical, and cultural questions. Sample topics: "Hellenistic Greek," "Famous Speeches in Ancient Greek Texts," "Praise and Blame in the Greek Poetic Tradition," "Tall Tales from Homer to Lucian." Prerequisite: Greek 231 or equivalent. Offered every third year.
