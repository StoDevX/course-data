SOAN 268: Class, Status, and Power (2012-2013, Semester 2)

How does social class stratification shape individuals' life chances in the U.S.? This course introduces students to classical and contemporary theories of social class; seminal empirical studies that investigate the power of social class in shaping both individuals and the nature of the society in which they live; the relationship between class, race, and gender; poverty and public policy; and class consciousness and class conflict. Prerequisite: Sociology/Anthropology 121 or another 100-or 200-level sociology/anthropology course. Offered annually.
