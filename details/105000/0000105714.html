FAMST 232: Introduction to Family Studies (2016-2017, Semester 1)

Students examine American families through the life cycle in relation to personal and professional life, with the major emphasis on communication and commitment in interpersonal relationships. Students study courtship, marriage, and adjustment to parenthood.They examine stressors that affect families such as family violence, divorce, grief and loss, and socioeconomic issues. This course takes an interdisciplinary approach; students have opportunity for a broad range of individualized study. Offered annually in the fall semester. Counts toward American studies major and family studies and management studies concentrations.
