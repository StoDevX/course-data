NURS 311: Foundations of Professional Nursing (2016-2017, Semester 1)

This course introduces the philosophical and scientific foundations of professional nursing. Students examine historical foundations of the nursing profession and scientific processes and frameworks underlying nursing theory and practice. Exploration of concepts in the nursing discipline builds knowledge and understanding essential to the provision of holistic patient care across the lifespan. Emphasis is on development of professionalism, use of critical thinking, and evidence-based practice. Counts toward the nursing major. Offered annually in the fall semester. Prerequisite: open to junior nursing majors only. Previous or concurrent registration with NURS 211. Concurrent registration with NURS 312 and NURS 313.
