GERM 251: Understanding Narratives (2006-2007, Semester 2)

Students Examine fictional works such as short stories, novel excerpts, and other prose texts, including film, with an eye to understanding how they work. Students retell stories in oral and written form and adapt them to different audiences. Coursework includes analysis of plot, characterization, style and point of view, review of cases, tenses and word order, as well as regular group conversations with native speakers. Taught in German. Prerequisite: German 232 or equivalent.
