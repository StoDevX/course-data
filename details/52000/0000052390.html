SPAN 276: Spanish as a First and Second Language (2006-2007, Semester 2)

Students explore the processes involved in the acquisition of Spanish as a first and second language and the variation present in the language of both native and non-native speakers of Spanish from Spain, Latin America, and the U.S. It features the study of Hispanic linguistics, with special attention paid to the socio-cultural contexts in which it is used as well as its structural aspects. Includes the study of at least one substantive literary work and a pronunciation lab. Taught in Spanish. Prerequisite: Spanish 250.
