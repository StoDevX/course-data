SPAN 315: Comparative "Hispanidades" (2006-2007, Semester 1)

Explores a topic pertinent to more than one geographic area of the Spanish-speaking world (Spain, Latin America, and the Hispanic U.S.). Students focus on comparative analysis through reading, discussion, and writing in Spanish. Sample topics include: Dictatorship and Literature; Patterns of Colonialism; Language and Identity; and Human Rights and Social Justice. Students may register more than once provided a different topic is offered. Includes the study of at least one substantive literary work. Taught in Spanish. Prerequisite: Spanish 250 and at least one 270-level course.
