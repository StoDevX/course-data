NURS 386: Perinatal Health (0.25) (2014-2015, Semester 1)

This course explores the childbearing process in healthy and high-risk families. Clinical experiences are provided with families during pregnancy, labor and delivery, and following birth. Clinical settings include hospital birth centers, clinics, simulation, and home care agencies. Students develop the role of educator by working with parents to integrate and care for a new member of the family system. Prerequisite: NURS 336. Offered annually in the fall semester. Counts toward women's and gender studies major and concentration.
