LATIN 377: Latin Satire (2005-2006, Semester 2)

The Romans claimed that satire was a literary genre of their own creation. Students are able to weigh the merits of that claim as they read selections from the wry and witty poems of prominent Roman satirists such as Persius, Horace, Martial and Juvenal. Prerequisite: Latin 231 or equivalent. GE: FOL-L. Offered every third year.
