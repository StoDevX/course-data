STAT 263B: Statistical Problem Solving (2005-2006, Semester 2)

This course emphasizes skills necessary to understand and analyze data. Topics include descriptive statistics, probability, and random variables, sampling theory, estimation, and classical hypothesis testing, practical and theoretical understanding of simple and multiple regression analysis. Applications to economics and business problems use real data, realistic applications, and Minitab for Windows. Written reports link statistical theory and practice with communication of results. Prerequisite: Mathematics 119, 120, 122 and one of Economics 110-122, or consent of the instructor. Offered each semester.
