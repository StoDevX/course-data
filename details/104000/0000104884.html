ASIAN 233: Nature in Japanese Literature and Culture (2015-2016, Semester 2)

This course examines how nature is imagined, described, and consumed in Japanese literature and culture, and considers moments when the natural environment becomes inhospitable to human life. Students explore the relationships between humans and nature in Japanese literature and popular culture by: (1) tracing traditions from eigth-century landscape poetry to post-apocalyptic narratives in contemporary anime, (2) reading Japanese literature from a perspective of ecological criticism, and (3) producing their own works of nature writing. Counts toward Asian studies major and Asian studies and Japan studies concentrations. Prerequisite: FYW.
